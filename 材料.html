Metal : Aluminum is a common metal used in daily life. It's found in kitchen utensils, cans, foils, and even in the construction of airplanes and buildings due to its light weight and resistance to corrosion.
Plastic : Polyethylene is the most common type of plastic. It's used in a variety of products, from plastic bags to water bottles, toys, and even bulletproof vests. It's known for its durability and flexibility.
1.Common materials in daily life
Graphene : This is a new material that is a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a two-dimensional honeycomb lattice. It's known for its exceptional strength, electrical conductivity, and flexibility.
Self-healing concrete: This is a new type of concrete that can repair its own cracks. It contains a specific type of bacteria that produces limestone when activated by water, effectively sealing any cracks that may appear.
Metal (Aluminum): Anodizing is a common post-processing method for aluminum. It increases resistance to corrosion and wear, and provides better adhesion for paint primers and glues. - Plastic (Polyethylene): Injection molding is a common post-processing method for polyethylene. It involves heating the plastic until it becomes liquid, then injecting it into a mold where it cools and solidifies. -
Wood: Sanding is a common post-processing method for wood. It smooths the surface of the wood, removing any imperfections and preparing it for finishing with paint or varnish.
